I would like to know what a sequence diagram is please 

ChatGPT
Certainly! A sequence diagram is a type of interaction diagram that shows how processes or objects operate with one another and in what order. It depicts the sequence of messages exchanged between different entities (objects or components) within a system to achieve a specific functionality.

Here are some key elements of a sequence diagram:

Lifelines: Lifelines represent the entities involved in the interaction, such as objects, components, or actors. They are typically depicted as vertical lines.
Messages: Messages are the communication events between lifelines. They show the flow of information or control between entities. There are different types of messages, including synchronous (blocking) and asynchronous (non-blocking).
Activation Boxes: Activation boxes represent the time during which an object is performing an action. They are drawn on top of a lifeline and show the duration of an object's activity.
Focus of Control: The focus of control indicates the period during which an object is actively engaged in processing.
Return Messages: Return messages show the flow of control back to the sender after an operation is completed.
Sequence diagrams are commonly used in software engineering to model the dynamic aspects of a system, illustrating how objects collaborate to fulfill a particular use case or scenario. They provide a visual representation of the chronological order of interactions, helping developers understand the behavior of a system during runtime.
